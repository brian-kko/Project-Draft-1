New York is the most densely populated city in the United States,
with over 29,000 people per square mile. Our residents produce
12,000 tons of waste daily, and more than 14 million tons are produced in our city annually.

Approximately 80 percent of our trash end up in landfills, and the
remainder ends up in incinerators or composting and recycling plants.
In landfills, garbage takes several decades to decades to break down.
Organic waste are also unable to decompose naturally, causing the
release of methane gas.

Trash and litter in our streets also end up in our waterways and oceans
through storm drains and sewers. 5 billion gallons of waste flows into
the East River each year, and the Hudson River is generally considered
unsafe to swim in due to the presence of man-made chemicals and pathogens.
